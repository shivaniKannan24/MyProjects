Organic Fertilizer

These are derived from natural sources, including plant or animal matter, and often contain a variety of nutrients. Examples include compost, manure, bone meal, seaweed, and fish emulsion. Organic fertilizers help improve soil structure and microbial activity while providing nutrients to plants in a slow-release manner.

Chemical Fertilizer

Also known as synthetic or inorganic fertilizers, these are manufactured using chemical processes. They typically contain specific concentrations of essential nutrients like nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium (N-P-K) in various forms that are easily absorbed by plants. Examples include ammonium nitrate, urea, and superphosphate. Chemical fertilizers deliver nutrients quickly to plants but may have the potential to cause environmental concerns like nutrient runoff.

Details
Mineral or Natural Fertilizers

These are derived from naturally occurring mineral sources and are less processed than chemical fertilizers. Examples include rock phosphate, greensand, and limestone. These fertilizers contain minerals and nutrients necessary for plant growth, and their release may occur slowly over time.
